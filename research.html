Research



The school of Computer Science is always trying to renovate and find solutions to the real world's problems. With the help of many award-winning staff and passionate postgraduate students, the school ventured into the four sciences; Physics, Math, Biology and Chemistry. We can all hope to achieve much more in the four sciences by using the ever-growing power of computers and their simulation abilities. Here are four of the research groups that we believe illustrates the great heights that computers can achieve.

BHI

BHI stands for Bio-Health Informatics. The group is interested in bringing our knowledge in genetics and biology with computer processing, as the raw, complex data such as DNA and protein chains. One of their projects was to use Taverna, a software program that analyzes biological data that would otherwise be very difficult to interpret. With their knowledge they could find the two genes that could stop the transition of the tsetse disease to cattle, which causes them to stop being productive. This could help save the millions of African farmers four billion American dollars.[3]
